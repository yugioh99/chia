Economic agents

Economic agents are the decision-makers in an economy—individuals, households, firms (businesses), governments, and institutions—who allocate resources, consume, produce, save, and invest, shaping economic outcomes through their choices, operating under scarcity, and responding to incentives. These actors are fundamental to understanding how markets and economies function, influencing everything from personal spending to national policies.
