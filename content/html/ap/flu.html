Deep dive

Fluid dynamics is the study of how liquids and gases (fluids) move, analyzing properties like velocity, pressure, and density to understand phenomena from weather patterns and ocean currents to airflow over aircraft and blood flow in bodies.

Physicists use the analogy of spacetime as a fluid, particularly a superfluid, as a powerful model to understand gravity and quantum mechanics, suggesting spacetime isn't fundamental but emerges from underlying components, much like water's properties emerge from molecules. However, it's crucial to remember this is a mathematical analogy for specific behaviors (analog gravity), not proof spacetime is a literal liquid with all its properties, as it still behaves like a fabric (Einstein's theory[1]) in other ways.
